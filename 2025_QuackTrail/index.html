Quack Trails

An Arduino-powered game of chance designed, programmed, and built from scratch.

View project brief Photo Gallery

Project Brief

Quack Trails began as a project for my AP Statistics class, where I wanted to turn concepts from probability into an interactive game. Players guide a duck named Steve along a trail, with each stage determined by chance. Before every round, players decide whether to spend tokens to continue, knowing that Steve may survive the next obstacle, or meet an unfortunate fate. Successful players earned small prizes and classroom rewards during our school’s annual fair.

I designed every aspect of the project, from the user interface and gameplay mechanics to the underlying probability model and reward system. The game was programmed in C++ on an Arduino Uno with a TFT display and custom button controls. This was my first substantial programming project and introduced me to embedded systems, event-driven programming, interface design, and translating mathematical ideas into an engaging physical experience. The game became one of the more popular attractions at the school fair, demonstrating how thoughtful interaction design can make abstract concepts approachable and entertaining.
