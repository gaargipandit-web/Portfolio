Cape Cod Residence

Designing a traditional Cape Cod home by transforming client requirements into a complete BIM model.

View project

Designed as part of my Civil Engineering and Architecture course, this project challenged me to create a home for a fictional client while satisfying a detailed set of architectural and functional requirements. The design had to follow the traditional Cape Cod style, provide approximately 4,000 square feet of living space, accommodate two adult sisters, their teenage niece, and their cat, and incorporate numerous client requests into a cohesive residence. Using Autodesk Revit and a Building Information Modeling (BIM) workflow, I developed the house from concept through complete architectural documentation, including floor plans, elevations, sections, and presentation drawings. Throughout the process, I balanced the client’s requirements with my own design ideas, creating an open-concept floor plan that improves circulation while preserving the recognizable character of Cape Cod architecture. The exterior incorporates hallmark architectural elements such as a steep gabled roof, dormer windows, a central chimney, and a symmetrical front façade. Inside, I introduced several custom features, including Jack-and-Jill bathrooms, a spiral staircase leading to a basement hobby room, and an open living area, to enhance functionality without departing from the traditional style. This project strengthened my understanding of client-centered design, residential planning, and the use of professional BIM tools to translate design concepts into complete architectural drawings.
