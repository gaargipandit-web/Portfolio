Queen Anne Residence

Studying historical architecture through original design and physical model making.

View project brief Photo Gallery

Project Brief

Inspired by the rich architectural vocabulary of the Queen Anne style, this project explores how historical design principles can inform an original residential design. Rather than replicating an existing building, I developed my own floor plan while incorporating defining Queen Anne elements such as the hexagonal tower, steep gabled roofs, dormer attic windows, wraparound porches, terraces, and layered rooflines. The resulting design balances historical authenticity with original spatial planning, demonstrating how architectural language can be adapted to create something new.

The interior layout was designed from the ground up to support modern living while preserving the character of the architectural style. The residence features a spacious entry foyer, a grand staircase, formal living and dining spaces, a kitchen, three bedrooms, three bathrooms, and a second-floor library located within the tower. Developing both the floor plan and the exterior simultaneously strengthened my understanding of the relationship between spatial organization, circulation, and architectural form.

The physical model was constructed using foam board for the structural walls and recycled corrugated cardboard strips for the exterior siding, while intricate decorative trims were digitally modeled in Fusion 360 and fabricated on an Ender-3 3D printer. Combining traditional craftsmanship with digital fabrication allowed for a high level of architectural detail and precision. This project received an Honorable Mention in the 2024 Scholastic Art & Writing Awards in the Design category and marked an important step in my exploration of architectural design, model making, and digital manufacturing.
