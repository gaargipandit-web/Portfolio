Project Brief

This project explored the design of a functional desktop organizer through parametric CAD modeling and additive manufacturing. The objective was to create a product with geometric complexity while maintaining manufacturability and everyday usability.

Inspired by the familiar form of a ladybug, I developed a pencil holder that combines playful aesthetics with practical function. The curved shell was modeled using revolved geometry and patterned features in Fusion 360, creating multiple openings for pencils while preserving the recognizable silhouette of the insect.

The model was fabricated on an Ender-3 3D printer using separate PLA prints for the red shell and black base, which were assembled after printing. The finished piece was presented as a personalized birthday gift, demonstrating how digital fabrication can transform a functional object into something meaningful and personal.

The design draws from the iconic geometry of a ladybug, simplifying its rounded shell and distinctive spotted pattern into a clean, functional desktop object. Bold color contrast and smooth organic forms give the pencil holder a friendly appearance while maintaining a balanced, manufacturable design.

Nature-Inspired Product Design

Transforms the recognizable form of a ladybug into a functional desktop accessory while preserving its playful visual identity.

Parametric CAD Modeling in Fusion 360

Developed as a fully parametric 3D model, allowing precise control over dimensions, geometry, and manufacturability.

Revolve and Circular Pattern Workflows

Utilizes revolved profiles and circular pattern tools to efficiently create the curved shell and evenly spaced pencil openings.

Multi-Part 3D Printed Assembly

Designed as separate components for dual-color printing in PLA, then assembled to achieve a clean, finished appearance.

Functional Desktop Organization

Provides organized storage for pencils, pens, and small office supplies while maintaining a compact footprint.

Personalized Gift Design

Created as a custom birthday gift, demonstrating how digital fabrication can produce meaningful, one-of-a-kind products.
