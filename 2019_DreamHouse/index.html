An early exploration of architectural design through hand-drawn plans

Project Brief

Designed in sixth grade as part of mathematics assignment on polygons, this project documents one of my earliest explorations into architecture and spatial design. Beginning with hand-drawn floor plans and a five-point perspective sketch, I developed a complete concept for my ideal home before translating it into a detailed physical model.

The design centers around an open courtyard that connects the living spaces while separating the public and private areas of the house. Constructing the model introduced me to architectural planning, circulation, proportions, and the relationship between two-dimensional drawings and three-dimensional form. Because I was too young to safely use precision cutting tools, the foam board pieces were cut by my teachers and family members following my measurements and assembly instructions, while I completed the design, planning, detailing, and construction of the model.

Looking back, this project marked the beginning of my interest in architecture and product design by combining creativity with structured problem solving.
