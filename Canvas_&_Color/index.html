This painting captures a fleeting moment of pure movement, a dancer caught mid-spin, her dress unfurling like a wave. I wanted to express not just the physical motion but the emotion that fuels it: joy, freedom, and the quiet strength in grace.

The figure is faceless, inviting viewers to see themselves in her place, to recall a time they felt entirely present in their own bodies. The flowing strokes of blue, green, and white in the dress were applied with a palette knife, creating texture that mirrors the energy of the dance. Against the dark, warm background, the movement becomes even more luminous, as if light itself is drawn to her rhythm.

This piece was sold at the Rochester Contemporary Art Center as part of their fundraiser.

Medium: Oil on Canvas (6”x6”)

This painting is an imagined re-visioning of the James Webb Space Telescope, blending science, surrealism, and symbolism. In place of its usual golden mirrors, each hexagon contains an eye human, animal, or imagined turning the telescope into a cosmic observer made of many perspectives.

I wanted to explore the idea that the search for knowledge is not purely mechanical; it is deeply human. Every image the telescope sends back is filtered through the collective vision of humanity, our curiosity, our hopes, and our interpretations. By replacing the mirrors with eyes, I transformed the instrument into something almost alive, as if the universe is being witnessed not by a single machine, but by countless conscious beings.

The swirling galactic background reflects the mystery and immensity of space, painted with layered brushstrokes to evoke depth and movement. The bright stars punctuate the darkness, representing moments of discovery. The gold of the mirrors stands out boldly, a reminder of the technology’s brilliance, while the eyes suggest that exploration is as much about looking inward as outward.

This piece represents one of my earliest experiences working with oil paint. The composition, arranged by my teacher, challenged me to explore the texture and depth that oil paint can provide. This still life allowed me to practice blending colors, working with a slow-drying medium, and capturing the fine details of the objects.

Medium: Oil on Canvas (8”x10”)

This abstract piece explores the dynamic interplay of color and form, using bold, unblended hues and thick black outlines to create a composition that is both vibrant and fluid. Inspired by the expressive qualities of Abstract Expressionism and Fauvism, the painting captures a sense of motion and energy, with shapes that seem to flow and interlock like pieces of stained glass.

The composition, with its swirling patterns and contrasting colors, invites the viewer to interpret the forms individually. The structured yet organic layout evokes a sense of spontaneity, paying homage to the modernist techniques of artists like Kandinsky and Matisse.

This piece was commissioned by my school teacher for the school auditorium. They specifically requested painting in Fauvism art movement style.
