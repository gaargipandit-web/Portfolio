Modular Doll

A modular doll with interchangable parts

View project brief Drawings Renderings Photo Gallery

Project Brief

This project explores the design of a modular 3D-printed doll with interchangeable parts and customizable accessories. Inspired by the flexibility of action figures and construction toys, the design focuses on durability, articulation, and personalization. Through multiple iterations of sketching, CAD modeling, prototyping, and testing, the project evolved into a system of detachable components connected through custom-designed joints that balance movement, strength, and ease of assembly.

Modular Figure Architecture

Limbs, accessories, and body components can be detached and replaced, allowing the figure to be customized and reconfigured.

Durable Articulated Joints

A magnet-assisted hinge system was developed as an alternative to traditional ball-and-socket joints, reducing breakage while preserving a wide range of motion.

Optimized for Additive Manufacturing

Every component was optimized for additive manufacturing, with multiple prototype iterations used to improve strength, printability, and assembly.

Interchangeable Character Attachments

The figure supports interchangeable attachments, including hairstyles, horns, jetpacks, wings, and future character-specific accessories.
