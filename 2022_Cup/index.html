Project Brief

This project represents one of my earliest explorations into computer-aided design and the development of a personal design language. Rather than recreating a conventional coffee mug, I wanted to reinterpret an everyday object using simple geometric forms, softened edges, and balanced proportions. The result is a minimalist square cup and matching coaster that blend functional design with a clean, contemporary aesthetic. Designing the object entirely in Fusion 360 introduced me to the relationship between geometry, usability, and visual identity, laying the foundation for the way I approach design today.

The project was also my first experience taking an idea through the complete digital fabrication workflow—from concept sketches and CAD modeling to physical prototyping on an Ender-3 3D printer using PLA filament. Every feature, including the rounded corners, wall thickness, handle geometry, and matching coaster, was designed from scratch and refined through multiple prototypes. These iterations highlighted the importance of manufacturability, print tolerances, and the subtle adjustments required to transform a digital model into a functional object.

Looking back, this project became an important milestone in my design journey. Beyond learning CAD software, it taught me that successful products are defined by a consistent design language, careful attention to proportion, and iterative refinement. The ideas explored here—minimal geometry, cohesive forms, and the integration of aesthetics with functionality—continue to influence my later architectural models, product concepts, and engineering projects.
